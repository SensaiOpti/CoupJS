Objective

To eliminate the influence of all other players and be the last survivor.

Influence

Face down cards in front of a player represent the player's influences and abilities.

Each time a player loses an influence, they must turn over and reveal a face down card of their choice. Revealed cards no longer provide influence, nor abilities. Revealed cards remain face up in front of the player, visible to everyone.

When a player has lost all of their influences, they are eliminated. The last player surviving wins.

Game Play

The game is played in turns. On a player's turn, they must choose one action only to play - a player may not pass.

After the action is chosen, other players have an opportunity to challenge or counteract that action. If an action is not challenged nor counteracted, the action automatically succeeds.

Challenges are resolved first before any action or counteraction is resolved.

When a player has lost all their influence and both their cards are face up in front of them, they are eliminated from the game. These cards are left face up.

The game ends when there is only player left.

Actions

A player may choose any action they want and can afford.

Certain actions are available to all players, regardless of influence cards. These are called 'General Actions' and are always available.

Other actions require claiming an influence: these are called 'Character Actions.' If a player chooses a Character Action, that player must claim that the required character is one of their face down cards. The player may be telling the truth or they may be bluffing. The player does not need to reveal any of their face down cards unless they are challenged. If they are not challenged, the player automatically succeeds in their chosen action.

General Actions

Income

Take 1 coin from the treasury. Cannot be blocked or challenged.

Foreign Aid

Take 2 coins from the treasury. Can be blocked by the Duke.

Coup

Pay 7 coins to force a player to lose influence. Cannot be blocked or challenged.

Required at 10+ coins: You MUST perform a Coup if you have 10 or more coins.

Blocks & Challenges

Blocks

Blocking works like other Character Actions in that players may claim an influence to block another player's action. The blocking player may be telling the truth or bluffing. Similar to Character Actions, no cards need be shown unless challenged. If left unchallenged, the block automatically succeeds.

Challenges

Any action in which a player claims a particular influence can be challenged.

If a player is challenged, they must prove they have the required influence by showing the relevant character is one of their face down cards. If they cannot prove it, or choose not to, they lose the challenge. If they can prove they have the required influence, the challenger loses.

Whoever loses the challenge immediately loses an influence, revealing a face down card.

If a player wins a challenge by showing the relevant influence, they return that card to the deck, shuffle, and draw a random replacement card. If an action is successfully challenged, the entire action fails; any coins paid as the cost of the action are returned to the player.

Any other player can issue a challenge to a player regardless of their involvement in the action.

Strategy Tips

  • You can claim any character action, even if you don't have that card (but you risk being challenged!)
  • Bluffing is a key part of the game
  • Pay attention to what other players claim and what they've been challenged on
  • Sometimes it's worth challenging even if you're not sure
  • Save coins for a Coup when other players are getting too powerful
  • Remember: you MUST Coup at 10 coins
