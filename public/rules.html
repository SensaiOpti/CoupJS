Objective

Be the last player with influence in the game. You start with 2 influence cards (characters) and 2 coins. When you lose both influence, you're eliminated.

Characters (Influence)

Duke

Action: Tax - Take 3 coins from the treasury

Blocks: Foreign Aid

Assassin

Action: Assassinate - Pay 3 coins to force a player to lose influence

Blocks: Nothing

Captain

Action: Steal - Take 2 coins from another player

Blocks: Stealing

Ambassador

Action: Exchange - Draw 2 cards, choose which to keep, return 2

Blocks: Stealing

Contessa

Action: None

Blocks: Assassination

Inquisitor (Expansion)

The Inquisitor replaces the Ambassador when the expansion is enabled.

Inquisitor

Action: Exchange - Draw 1 card, choose to keep it or your current card

Action: Examine - Look at one of another player's cards. You may force them to exchange it

Blocks: Stealing

General Actions

These actions are available to all players, regardless of their influence cards:

Income

Take 1 coin from the treasury. Cannot be blocked or challenged.

Foreign Aid

Take 2 coins from the treasury. Can be blocked by the Duke.

Coup

Pay 7 coins to force a player to lose influence. Cannot be blocked or challenged.

Required at 10+ coins: You MUST perform a Coup if you have 10 or more coins.

Challenge & Block

Challenge

When a player claims a character action, any other player may challenge them.

If the challenge succeeds: The player didn't have the character they claimed. They lose one influence and the action fails.

If the challenge fails: The player reveals the character card they claimed, returns it to the deck, draws a new card, and the challenger loses one influence. The action then proceeds.

Block

When certain actions are taken, players can claim to block them with the appropriate character.

Targeted blocks (Steal, Assassinate): Only the target of the action can block.

Untargeted blocks (Foreign Aid): Any player can block.

Challenging blocks: Any player can challenge a block. The challenge works the same way as challenging an action.

Turn Order

  1. On your turn, choose one action to perform
  2. If the action can be challenged, other players may challenge
  3. If the action can be blocked, the target (or any player for Foreign Aid) may block
  4. If a block is claimed, any player may challenge the block
  5. The action resolves if not successfully challenged or blocked
  6. Play passes to the next player

Losing Influence

When you lose influence, you must choose one of your face-down cards to reveal. That card is now out of play.

When both your cards are revealed, you're eliminated from the game.

Winning

The last player with at least one face-down influence card wins!

Strategy Tips

  • You can claim any character action, even if you don't have that card (but you risk being challenged!)
  • Bluffing is a key part of the game
  • Pay attention to what other players claim and what they've been challenged on
  • Sometimes it's worth challenging even if you're not sure
  • Save coins for a Coup when other players are getting too powerful
  • Remember: you MUST Coup at 10 coins
